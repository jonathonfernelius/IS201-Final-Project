Starting Consonants (พยัญชนะต้น)

In Thai, every consonant has one of three classes, these are primarily used by the first letter in each syllable (the พยัญชนะต้น) and are used to determine the tone in that syllable. I will not go into all the tonal rules in this page, however I will tell you the classes of each letter in the Thai alphabet.

ClassLetters
Low Class (อักษรต่ำ) ค ฅ ฆ ง ช ซ ฌ ญ ฑ ฒ ณ ท ธ น พ ฟ ภ ม ย ร ล ว ฬ ฮ
Middle Class (อักษรกลาง) ก จ ฎ ฏ ด ต บ ป อ
High Class (อักษรสูง) ข ฃ ฉ ฐ ถ ผ ฝ ศ ษ ส ห

Vowels (สระ)

In Thai, there are long vowels and short vowels. Usually these come in pairs such as -า (kind of a more drawn out a/u) and -ะ (a really short a/u). Knowing the sound and length is obviously important to pronounce things correctly but it can also have an affect on a syllables tone. Additionally, if there is no ending consonant but the vowel is the last sound, a short vowel will (usually) produce a dead syllable while a long vowel makes it a living syllable (which I will talk about a bit more in the next section).

Thai reads left to right just like English however a vowel may be written before (or above or below) a consonant in the same syllable. When you learn Thai you need to learn to read as syllables rather than just a series of letters.

Ending Consonants (ตัวสะกด)

Worth noting is that consonants make a different noise depending on whether or not they are at the front of the back of syllable. While there are 21 possible sounds (there are more letters but several of them make the same sound) for the starting consonant, there are only 8 consonant ending noises (9 if you include ending with a vowel). Knowing whether or not a syllable is alive or dead is crucial to knowing the tone used in that syllable. Some of the Thai letters also simply can never be used at the end of a syllable.

Live Endings

Of the 8 possible final consonant sounds, 5 are alive. This means you can keep making that noise forever.

SoundLetters
แม่กน (n)น ณ ญ ร ล ฬ
แม่กม (m)ม
แม่เกย (y)ย
แม่เกอว (w)ว
แม่กง (ng)ง

Dead Endings

Of the 8 possible final consonant sounds, 3 are dead. While sometimes you will see the English letters g/k, b/p, and d/t used to symbolize the sounds, it is important to know that they are slightly different in pronunciation (you kind of cut off before you finish saying the letter).

SoundLetters
แม่กก (g/k)ก ข ค ฆ
แม่กบ (b/p)บ ป พ ภ ฟ
แม่กด (d/t)จ ด ต ถ ท ธ ฎ ฏ ฑ ฒ ช ซ ศ ษ ส
